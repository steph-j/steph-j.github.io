The World of Steph at EDA

Time and Habits Reflection

The techniques I tried were meditation, creating a habit and the Fogg Method

What I found interesting

I found all the readings and videos very interesting. Of particular interest was the Fogg Method of attaching a little new habit to an already existing habit. So when I do something like 'eat breakfast' then immediately I will do something small like 'meditate' for 2 minutes and build it up from there. We also need to reward ourselves after doing the new habit with a happy dance.

I had also never considered taking up meditation before but seeing the results of the MRI scan of a pre and post meditation brain was very enlightening. The article explained the benefits of meditation very well. I would like to lessen the stress reaction in my brain. Being able to strengthen my memory and focus for longer would also be definitely welcome.

It was interesting to learn that when we cultivate a habit the behaviour should come first and then the attitude will follow. This is backward to how I have thought of the relationship between the brain and our actions in the past.

What Worked

Piggy-backing my meditation with breakfast has worked so far.

Experimentation

I will definitely experiment with these three methods more. Especially the Fogg method and meditation.
